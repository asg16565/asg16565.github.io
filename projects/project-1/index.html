ATTRACTIONS

Hersheypark

A renowned amusement park featuring exciting rides, live entertainment, and an opportunity to learn about chocolate-making at Hershey's Chocolate World.

It is the largest amusement park in the state of Pennsylvania, offering thrilling roller coasters, family attractions, and an 11-acre water park.

Gettysburg National Military Park

Site of the pivotal Battle of Gettysburg during the Civil War, offering guided tours, historic monuments, and a visitor center with exhibits.

It was this momentous battle that inspired President Abraham Lincoln's immortal "Gettysburg Address".

Pennsylvania State Capitol

An impressive architectural gem in the city of Harrisburg showcasing exquisite artwork, stunning interiors, and guided tours to learn about the state's government and history.

Built and furnished at a cost of $13 million, the PA Capitol features paintings, stained glass, and furnishings by some of the best artisans of the day.

Pine Grove Furnace State Park

Serving as "Gateway to the Appalachian Trail," this park offers hiking, picnicking, and swimming opportunities, along with historic features and natural beauty.

The Appalachian Trail Museum Festival is held every June, featuring a banquet, hikes, guest speakers, music, and children's programs.

Worlds End State Park

Situated in the Endless Mountains, this park is known for its rugged beauty, with hiking trails, waterfalls, and breathtaking views of the Loyalsock Creek Gorge.

Scenery is particularly unforgettable during the June mountain laurel bloom and the fall foliage period in October.

The Pennsylvania Grand Canyon

Also known as Pine Creek Gorge, this natural wonder features stunning vistas, hiking trails, and opportunities for outdoor recreation.

An environmental educator presents resource-oriented programs and nature walks April through October.
